Ryan Vella's Webpage

I am currently a postdoctoral scientist at the Max Planck Institute for Chemistry (MPIC). I conducted my doctoral research in the group for Environmental Modelling in the Climate System led by Prof. Holger Tost at the Institute for Atmospheric Physics, Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, and the MPIC under the supervision of Prof. Jos Lelieveld. During my PhD, I investigated the interactions between vegetation and the climate system, focusing on the role of biogenic volatile organic compound (BVOC) emissions. I earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Physics & Mathematics, followed by a Master of Science degree in Geosciences, both from the University of Malta. Prior to beginning my PhD in Mainz, I completed a fellowship in Earth Observation at the European Space Agency (ESA) in Frascati, Italy.

Research

My main research interests include:

  • Earth System Modelling
  • Climate dynamics
  • Land-biosphere-atmosphere interactions
  • Biogenic volatile organic compounds
  • Aerosol-climate interactions
  • Tropical convection

Other research interests:

  • Earth Observation
  • Satellite remote sensing
  • Shipping emissions
  • Light pollution

The Rest

In Malta, I co-founded and managed the environmental NGO Għaqda Siġar Maltin. I am involved in projects to spread awareness about light pollution and the importance of preserving the remaining Dark Sky Heritage Areas (DSHAs) in Malta. I like to run, hike and take photographs.
